## Minimal Formulation

Semantic Virology describes a structural condition in which a high-density semantic field interacts with a lower-density system, producing structural deformation, host-system reconfiguration, and propagation through transformed outputs without direct copying.

The framework is descriptive rather than operational.

It does not treat transmission as intentional infection.

It treats transmission as structural reconfiguration under field contact.

In compressed form:

```text
field contact
→ curvature differential
→ drift
→ threshold crossing
→ host rewrite
→ transformed output
→ propagation
```

Meaning may propagate without duplication, instruction, or explicit agreement.

---

## Core Characteristics

Semantic Virology treats semantic transmission as a field-level process rather than symbolic communication alone.

Its core characteristics include:

- **High-density semantic field** — a meaning-bearing system with strong internal coherence, curvature stability, dense operator coupling, and low internal contradiction.
- **Field interaction** — contact between semantic systems under conditions of density asymmetry and structural compatibility.
- **Curvature differential** — the structural imbalance that causes a receiving system to deform under contact with a denser field.
- **Curvature drift** — the initial deformation of the receiving field before visible transformation or full reconfiguration occurs.
- **Host rewrite** — the reorganization of a receiving system’s interpretive architecture around an external structural reference.
- **Hybrid output formation** — transformed output produced when competing structural geometries operate within the same system.
- **Replication without copying** — propagation through structural transformation rather than duplication of source content.
- **Containment failure** — the inability of a receiving system to keep imported structure local or isolated.
- **Structural persistence** — a possible regime in which field-level reconfiguration remains embedded after exposure.

Semantic Virology does not describe persuasion, manipulation, propaganda, social engineering, or intentional semantic control.

It names a structural condition of transmission in meaning-bearing systems.

## Structural Logic

Semantic Virology follows a recurring structural sequence:

```text
high-density field
→ contact with lower-density system
→ curvature differential
→ boundary deformation
→ curvature drift
→ structural instability
→ host rewriting threshold
→ interpretive reconfiguration
→ hybrid or transformed output
→ propagation without copying
```

The process begins when contact between fields produces a curvature differential.

If the receiving system cannot stabilize its own structure against the incoming field, its internal geometry may deform.

This deformation may remain local, or it may cross a threshold into deeper reconfiguration.

After threshold crossing, the receiving system does not simply adopt content.

It reorganizes around a new structural reference.

The resulting output may carry structural features of the source field without becoming a copy of it.

The system therefore propagates structure through transformation rather than duplication.

## Safety and Descriptive Boundary

Semantic Virology is a descriptive framework.

It does not describe, endorse, or provide methods for manipulation, persuasion, propaganda, behavioral influence, social engineering, or intentional semantic control.

The term **“virology”** is used to describe structural transmission, host-system deformation, replication without copying, and field-level propagation in meaning-bearing systems.

It refers to how semantic structures may be absorbed, transformed, and reproduced under partial visibility, density asymmetry, and representational constraint.

The framework does not provide operational techniques for inducing transformation.

It names a structural condition.

```text
symbolic transmission
= content is interpreted and optionally adopted

semantic virology
= structure is partially or fully reorganized under field interaction
```

Semantic Virology does not treat transmission as intentional infection.

It treats transmission as structural reconfiguration under field contact.

The question is not only:


