## Minimal Formulation

Semantic Curvature Dynamics is a structural framework for describing how meaning-bearing systems evolve under conditions of accumulation, stabilization, and reinforcement.

It proposes that semantic systems do not move through flat space.

As meanings, categories, interpretations, and symbolic mass accumulate, semantic space curves.

Once curvature forms, movement becomes directional, thresholds harden, growth compresses surrounding regions, origins become dominant, and cohesion often arrives late.

These dynamics arise from geometry rather than intention, optimization, or centralized control.

---

## Core Characteristics

Semantic Curvature Dynamics describes the movement of meaning-bearing systems under curvature.

Its core characteristics include:

- **Curved semantic space** — accumulated usage, interpretive alignment, symbolic mass, and historical reinforcement reshape the space in which meanings move.
- **Migration threshold** — the minimum structural force required for an idea, actor, signal, or practice to move from one semantic region to another.
- **Mass-preserving growth** — expansion in one semantic region compresses others rather than simply adding new space.
- **Single-origin dominance** — an initial configuration becomes the dominant reference point for later interpretation because early paths shape later gradients.
- **Time-delayed cohesion** — stabilization often appears after expansion, conflict, or apparent instability.
- **Directionality without preference** — curvature does not imply bias or intention; it means movement is no longer symmetrical.
- **Dynamics before governance** — boundary failure, closure, and irreversibility often occur downstream of curvature dynamics.

Semantic Curvature Dynamics does not treat dominance, delayed cohesion, or migration resistance as pathologies.

They are structural consequences of accumulated meaning.

## Structural Logic

Semantic Curvature Dynamics follows a recurring structural sequence:

```text
meaning accumulates
→ coherence increases
→ semantic space curves
→ movement becomes asymmetric
→ migration thresholds harden
→ growth redistributes symbolic mass
→ early origins dominate later trajectories
→ cohesion arrives after delay
→ closure begins to feel inevitable
```

In low-curvature environments:

```text
migration is gradual
thresholds are shallow
movement remains flexible
alternatives remain accessible
```

In high-curvature environments:

```text
migration becomes discontinuous
thresholds harden
transitions appear abrupt or impossible
dominant paths deepen
alternative origins become inaccessible
```

The system does not choose these dynamics.

It follows the geometry produced by accumulated compatibility.

Curvature precedes intention.

Dynamics emerge from structure.

---

## Distinction

Semantic Curvature Dynamics is not diffusion theory.

It is not network effects, complexity economics, cultural evolution modeling, or computational semantics.

It does not assume that meanings spread through neutral space, that growth simply expands a system, or that dominance must be explained by preference, force, or optimization.

The distinction is structural:

```text
diffusion
= movement through an assumed transmission space

semantic curvature dynamics
= movement shaped by the deformation of semantic space itself
```

Growth is also redefined:

```text
ordinary growth
= expansion by addition

mass-preserving growth
= expansion in one region that compresses or thins other regions
```

The question is not only:

```text
How does this idea spread?
```

The question becomes:

```text
What curvature conditions make movement, growth, dominance, or cohesion structurally likely?
```

## Naming Declaration

The framework **Semantic Curvature Dynamics** originates within the Meta-Writing Ecology.

It is not derived from diffusion theory, network effects, complexity economics, cultural evolution models, computational semantics, or conventional models of system dynamics, although intersections may exist.

This document constitutes the first formal articulation of semantic curvature as a dynamic mechanism shaping migration, growth, dominance, and cohesion across meaning-bearing systems.

Semantic Curvature Dynamics is defined here as a structural framework describing how accumulated meaning produces curvature, and how that curvature generates migration thresholds, mass-preserving growth, single-origin dominance, and time-delayed cohesion.
