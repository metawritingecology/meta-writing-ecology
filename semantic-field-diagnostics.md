## Minimal Formulation

Semantic Field Diagnostics is a structural vocabulary for recognizing drift, projection, boundary substitution, and collapse within meaning-bearing systems.

It is not a method, toolkit, checklist, evaluative procedure, or intervention strategy.

It names structural states that constrain interpretation before observable failure occurs.

Systems rarely fail where they are observed.

By the time operational failure becomes visible, the semantic field in which interpretation and judgment occur may already have deformed.

---

## Core Characteristics

Semantic Field Diagnostics shifts attention from visible breakdown to upstream field deformation.

Its core characteristics include:

- **Semantic field** — the structured space in which concepts, categories, distinctions, and interpretations are organized and navigated.
- **Drift** — gradual misalignment in which meanings shift relative to their original reference without discrete rupture.
- **Projection** — internally generated structures are mistaken for external reality.
- **Boundary substitution** — representational boundaries replace substantive distinctions.
- **Collapse** — the semantic field loses the capacity to sustain distinction.
- **Diagnostics without methods** — diagnostics here means recognition of structural conditions, not procedural detection or correction.
- **Regime recognition** — drift, projection, substitution, and collapse are treated as structural regimes rather than isolated events.
- **AI-mediated field deformation** — semantic fields become partially externalized into durable representations, accelerating closure and projection.

Semantic Field Diagnostics does not diagnose behavior alone.

It recognizes the field conditions that shape what behavior, judgment, and coordination can mean.

## Structural Logic

Semantic Field Diagnostics follows a recurring structural sequence:

```text
semantic field forms
→ distinctions become available or unavailable
→ interpretations become admissible or inadmissible
→ ambiguity is tolerated or suppressed
→ field deformation begins
→ drift / projection / boundary substitution / collapse
→ operational failure is misdiagnosed downstream
```

A semantic field determines:

```text
what distinctions are available
which interpretations are admissible
how disagreement is expressed
where ambiguity is tolerated
```

When the field deforms, failure does not first appear as breakdown.

It appears as constrained perception.

The system may continue to operate.

It may remain coherent.

It may retain metrics, procedures, and outcomes.

But the space in which interpretation occurs has narrowed.

Failure becomes inevitable not because decisions are poor, but because the field in which decisions are made has already changed.

---

## Distinction

Semantic Field Diagnostics is not organizational diagnostics.

It is not evaluation theory, systems auditing, AI monitoring, performance review, or governance assessment.

It does not specify who should diagnose, how diagnosis should be operationalized, when diagnosis should occur, or what action should follow.

The distinction is structural:

```text
diagnostic procedure
= a method for detecting and correcting a problem

semantic field diagnostics
= a vocabulary for recognizing structural states that constrain interpretation
```

It also shifts the diagnostic question:

```text
ordinary diagnosis
= what went wrong in behavior, incentives, performance, or outcomes?

semantic field diagnostics
= what has happened to the field that makes certain interpretations possible or impossible?
```

The problem is not only that a system decides incorrectly.

The deeper problem is that the field may no longer allow the relevant distinction to appear.

## Naming Declaration

The framework **Semantic Field Diagnostics** and the diagnostic constructs articulated in this document originate within the Meta-Writing Ecology.

They are not derived from organizational diagnostics, evaluation theory, systems auditing, AI monitoring frameworks, epistemic assessment methodologies, or conventional governance diagnostics, although intersections may exist.

This document constitutes the first formal articulation of Semantic Field Diagnostics as a structural vocabulary for identifying drift, projection, boundary substitution, and collapse in meaning-bearing systems.

Semantic Field Diagnostics is defined here as a descriptive grammar for recognizing semantic field deformation without reducing recognition to method, metric, procedure, or intervention.
