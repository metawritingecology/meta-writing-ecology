## Minimal Formulation

Proxy Substitution is the structural condition in which a proxy ceases to represent an underlying referent and becomes the object of governance, evaluation, or optimization itself.

It describes the transition from representation to replacement.

The system does not merely mis-measure reality.

It reorients itself around the proxy, rendering the original referent structurally inaccessible, irrelevant, or non-recoverable.

---

## Core Characteristics

Proxy Substitution treats proxies as structurally necessary but unstable representational devices.

Its core characteristics include:

- **Proxy dependence** — complex systems rely on indicators, metrics, models, classifications, benchmarks, or algorithmic outputs to stand in for more complex realities.
- **Dimensional reduction** — proxies compress complexity into forms that can be acted upon, compared, optimized, or governed.
- **Referent recession** — once a proxy becomes visible and operationally useful, the underlying reality it represents begins to recede.
- **Proxy-relative evaluation** — success, failure, accountability, and optimization become defined in relation to proxy behavior.
- **Representation inversion** — the system begins to interpret reality through the proxy rather than using the proxy to refer outward.
- **Proxy collapse** — prolonged substitution produces a terminal condition in which the original referent can no longer be reconstructed.
- **AI-mediated intensification** — automation, model outputs, and algorithmic optimization accelerate substitution by giving proxies persistence, authority, and operational autonomy.

Proxy Substitution does not require flawed measurement, manipulation, corruption, bias, or bad faith.

It can occur under conditions of technical accuracy, procedural compliance, institutional legitimacy, and good intentions.

---

## Structural Logic

Proxy Substitution follows a recurring structural sequence:

```text
complex referent
→ proxy representation
→ proxy stabilization
→ proxy authority
→ governance around proxy behavior
→ referent recession
→ representation becomes replacement
→ proxy collapse
```

At first, the proxy points outward.

It helps the system coordinate around something too complex to govern directly.

Over time, the proxy becomes easier to see than the referent.

It becomes easier to measure.

It becomes easier to optimize.

It becomes easier to defend.

Eventually, the system no longer consults reality to validate the proxy.

Reality is interpreted through the proxy.

Representation becomes replacement.

---

## Distinction

Proxy Substitution is not ordinary measurement error.

It is not bias, gaming, manipulation, corruption, or misuse.

It is also distinct from decision drift and reference drift.

```text
decision drift
= misalignment between assumptions and execution

reference drift
= decay of the baseline that originally justified system operation

proxy substitution
= replacement of the object of governance by its representation
```

The distinction is structural:

```text
bad proxy
= the proxy represents poorly

proxy substitution
= the system governs the proxy instead of the referent
```

The question is not only:

```text
Is the proxy accurate?
```

The question becomes:

```text
Has the proxy become the thing the system is actually governing?
```

## Naming Declaration

The concepts **Proxy Substitution** and **Proxy Collapse**, and the structural framework articulated in this document, originate within the Meta-Writing Ecology.

They are not derived from principal–agent theory, performance management literature, AI alignment discourse, organizational control theory, metric governance frameworks, or conventional accounts of indicator failure, although intersections may exist.

This document constitutes the first formal articulation of Proxy Substitution as a structural governance failure arising from sustained representational dominance in meaning-bearing systems.

Proxy Substitution is defined here as the condition in which representation ceases to point outward and instead becomes the internal object of governance, evaluation, or optimization.
