## Minimal Formulation

Semantic Physics is a theoretical framework for analyzing meaning systems as relational fields.

It proposes that languages, narratives, beliefs, knowledge systems, and information environments do not evolve only through isolated symbols or individual interpretations. They develop as structured semantic fields shaped by density, pressure, curvature, observer interaction, and interpretive drift.

Rather than asking only what a symbol means, Semantic Physics asks how a meaning-bearing environment forms, stabilizes, bends, drifts, reorganizes, or collapses.

---

## Core Characteristics

Semantic Physics begins from the field rather than the isolated symbol.

Its core characteristics include:

- **Semantic fields** — relational environments where meanings, interpretations, narratives, and institutions interact.
- **Semantic density** — the concentration of meaning produced through repetition, reinforcement, cultural transmission, institutional use, or communicative recurrence.
- **Structural pressure** — tension generated when semantic density becomes unevenly distributed within a field.
- **Semantic curvature** — deformation of interpretive space caused by accumulated density and pressure.
- **Observer interaction** — the participation of individuals, institutions, communication systems, and algorithmic agents as embedded nodes within the field.
- **Interpretive drift** — gradual transformation of meaning through repeated interpretation, contextual change, translation, and reuse.
- **Recursive stabilization** — the tendency of meaning systems to reinforce their own interpretive rules, increasing density and limiting alternative trajectories.

Semantic Physics treats meaning as a dynamic environment rather than a static property.

---

## Structural Logic

Within Semantic Physics, meaning systems evolve through a recurring structural sequence:

```text
semantic field
→ semantic density
→ structural pressure
→ semantic curvature
→ observer interaction
→ drift / stabilization / transformation
```

Meanings do not simply appear and circulate.

They cluster.

They accumulate density.

They generate pressure.

They curve the space of interpretation.

They shape what later meanings can enter, resist, stabilize, or transform.

A semantic system is therefore not only a collection of signs.  
It is an evolving field of relations.

---

## Distinction

Semantic Physics is not a theory of individual word meaning.

It is not ordinary symbolic semantics, discourse analysis, information theory, cognitive linguistics, or a computational similarity model.

It does not treat meaning as something primarily located inside a word, sign, or isolated statement. Its primary unit of analysis is the relational structure of the semantic environment.

It also differs from generic or metaphorical uses of “semantic physics.” In the present framework, the term refers specifically to the structural dynamics of meaning-bearing systems: density, pressure, curvature, observer participation, drift, recursion, and transformation.

The question is not only:

```text
What does this symbol mean?
```

The question becomes:

```text
What kind of field allows this meaning to form, persist, bend, drift, or collapse?
```

## Naming Declaration

The framework **Semantic Physics** originates within the Meta-Writing Ecology.

In this context, Semantic Physics denotes a field-dynamic approach to meaning systems. It is not derived from conventional symbolic semantics, discourse analysis, information theory, cognitive linguistics, computational semantics, systems theory, or semantic similarity modeling, although intersections may exist.

This registration records the initial research program for Semantic Physics and provides a time-stamped formal reference for its conceptual foundation.

Semantic Physics is defined here as a framework for studying meaning environments as relational fields shaped by semantic density, structural pressure, curvature, observers, drift, recursion, and transformation.
