## Minimal Formulation

Premature Coherence is the systemic condition in which alignment, consistency, or symbolic closure emerges before a meaning-bearing system has accumulated sufficient differentiation, variation, or interpretive capacity.

It describes a form of apparent stability that conceals the loss of semantic resolution.

The system appears coherent not because tensions have been resolved, but because unresolved distinctions have been compressed, eliminated, or made structurally unavailable.

Coherence is not inherently stabilizing.

It is structurally timed.

---

## Core Characteristics

Premature Coherence treats coherence as a temporally sensitive structural force rather than a neutral sign of maturity or correctness.

Its core characteristics include:

- **Early closure** — coherence appears before semantic space has been sufficiently explored or differentiated.
- **Loss of semantic resolution** — fine-grained distinctions collapse before they can be tested, articulated, or stabilized.
- **Compression of alternatives** — unresolved possibilities become harder to maintain inside the system.
- **Alignment without resolution** — the system appears aligned because divergence has been suppressed, not because understanding has matured.
- **Stability through reduction** — clarity emerges by narrowing the interpretive field.
- **Irreversibility risk** — once distinctions are lost, later correction or recalibration becomes structurally costly or impossible.
- **AI-mediated acceleration** — generative and large-scale systems intensify premature coherence by rapidly stabilizing dominant patterns and suppressing low-frequency distinctions.

Premature Coherence does not require error, bias, manipulation, or bad faith.

It can emerge under ordinary pressures for decisiveness, efficiency, consistency, coordination, and legibility.

---

## Structural Logic

Premature Coherence follows a recurring structural sequence:

```text
pressure for alignment / consistency / decisiveness
→ reduced tolerance for variation
→ semantic space compresses
→ unresolved distinctions collapse
→ coherence appears
→ semantic resolution is lost
→ future interpretive paths narrow
```

The system does not become coherent because it has fully understood its complexity.

It becomes coherent because complexity has been reduced below the threshold of visible disturbance.

Coherence arrives.

Distinctions disappear.

The system stabilizes.

But the stability is produced by closure rather than resolution.

Under such conditions, a system may become more confident while becoming less capable of noticing what it no longer distinguishes.

---

## Distinction

Premature Coherence is not ordinary consensus.

It is not successful alignment.

It is not mature stability, resolved disagreement, or shared understanding.

It refers specifically to coherence that emerges before a system has developed enough semantic resolution to preserve meaningful distinctions.

It also differs from simple error or failure. Premature Coherence can occur while a system appears functional, efficient, stable, and institutionally successful.

The distinction is structural:

```text
earned coherence
= coherence after differentiation, testing, and resolution

premature coherence
= coherence before differentiation, produced through compression
```

The question is not only:

```text
Is the system coherent?
```

The question becomes:

```text
Did coherence arrive after resolution, or did it replace resolution?
```
