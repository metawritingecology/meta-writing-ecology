## Minimal Formulation

Zero-Field is the structural condition in which a system’s semantic field collapses toward minimal internal differentiation.

It does not describe emptiness, neutrality, balance, or reset.

It describes a state in which meaningful variation has been structurally eliminated, leaving binary, absolute, polarized, or totalizing configurations.

Zero-Field is not absence.

It is over-compression.

---

## Core Characteristics

Zero-Field treats extremism, rigidity, and paradoxical stability as structural consequences of semantic collapse.

Its core characteristics include:

- **Semantic null state** — a field condition in which internal differentiation has collapsed toward minimal variation.
- **Loss of intermediate states** — moderate, transitional, or ambiguous positions become structurally unavailable.
- **Extremism gradient** — small differences in input produce disproportionate reactions, categorical shifts, or absolute judgments.
- **Paradoxical stability** — the system appears stable because variation has already been eliminated.
- **Rigidity without depth** — the field becomes clear, simple, and coherent while losing semantic range.
- **Zero is not flatness** — a flat field permits movement; a Zero-Field collapses movement into polarity.
- **Downstream collapse** — Zero-Field emerges from prolonged coherence, boundary failure, representational compression, and stabilized dominance.
- **Misdiagnosis risk** — Zero-Field is often mistaken for radicalization, polarization, moral failure, or informational deficit.

Zero-Field is not an ideological, moral, or psychological condition.

It is a field condition.

---

## Structural Logic

Zero-Field follows a recurring structural sequence:

```text
prolonged coherence
→ representational compression
→ boundary failure
→ loss of intermediate variation
→ semantic gradients steepen
→ alternatives become unavailable
→ polarity dominates
→ paradoxical stability
```

In a flat semantic field:

```text
variation exists
transitions are possible
movement remains available
exploration can occur
```

In a Zero-Field:

```text
variation disappears
movement collapses into polarity
deviation is interpreted as opposition
alternatives are structurally unavailable
```

The system does not fragment.

It simplifies.

It does not become empty.

It becomes saturated without variation.

Extremes dominate not by force, but by geometry.

---

## Distinction

Zero-Field is not polarization theory.

It is not political extremism theory, moral psychology, conflict resolution theory, or an account of ideological radicalization.

It does not begin from actors, beliefs, moral commitments, or intentional coercion.

The distinction is structural:

```text
polarization
= opposed positions intensify within a field

zero-field
= the field itself loses intermediate differentiation
```

Zero-Field is also distinct from flatness:

```text
flat field
= low curvature with available movement

zero-field
= collapsed differentiation with movement constrained into polarity
```

The question is not only:

```text
Why are positions becoming extreme?
```

The question becomes:

```text
What happened to the semantic field such that intermediate variation disappeared?
```

## Naming Declaration

The concept **Zero-Field**, along with associated terms including **Extremism Gradient** and **Structural Non-Equivalence of Zero and Flatness**, originates within the Meta-Writing Ecology.

These concepts are not derived from political extremism theory, polarization studies, moral psychology, conflict resolution frameworks, or conventional accounts of radicalization, although intersections may exist.

This document constitutes the first formal articulation of Zero-Field as a phenomenal structural condition in meaning-bearing systems.

Zero-Field is defined here as the structural condition in which a system’s semantic field collapses toward minimal internal differentiation, producing extremism, rigidity, and paradoxical stability without requiring ideological intent or actor-level explanation.
